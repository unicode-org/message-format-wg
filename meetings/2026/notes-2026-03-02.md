## [Well-formed vs valid \#935](https://github.com/unicode-org/message-format-wg/issues/935)

Resolved to use “well-formed” for all value references external to MessageFormat itself.

### Datetime: Timezone

* [Don't include a time zone option, or be strict if you do \#961](https://github.com/unicode-org/message-format-wg/issues/961)  
* [Be more specific about time zone IDs \#962](https://github.com/unicode-org/message-format-wg/issues/962)

For timezones, this means relying on the first two [general guidelines used for choosing timezone names](https://data.iana.org/time-zones/theory.html#naming) to provide a definition of “well-formed”.

As the guidelines are not necessarily well enough defined to be used as a “well-formed” definition, we may want/need to either inline a definition, or to refer to e.g. the JS Temporal spec's definition.

### Datetime: Calendar

* [Resolution between input calendar and message calendar \#963](https://github.com/unicode-org/message-format-wg/issues/963)

For calendar identifiers, “well-formed” requires depending on the LDML [uvalue definition](https://unicode.org/reports/tr35/tr35.html#uvalue), which technically allows for `_` as a separator, even though that is never used by actual [Unicode Calendar Identifiers](https://unicode.org/reports/tr35/tr35.html#UnicodeCalendarIdentifier).

## [Restrict literals for :date and :time \#680](https://github.com/unicode-org/message-format-wg/issues/680)

Agreed to restrict :date operands to only date strings, and :time operands to only time strings.
